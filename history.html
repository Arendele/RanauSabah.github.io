About Ranau

The Ranau District is an administrative district located in the state of Sabah, Malaysia. It is part of the West Coast Division, which includes other districts such as Kota Belud, Kota Kinabalu, Papar, Penampang, Putatan, Ranau, and Tuaran. The district's capital is Ranau Town. According to the 2010 Census, the district had a population of 94,092, with the majority belonging to the Dusun ethnic community.

Ranau is noted for its hilly geographical structure and is the largest producer of highland vegetables in the state of Sabah. Ranau's diverse flora ranges from rich tropical lowland and hill rainforest to tropical mountain, sub-alpine forest and scrub on the higher elevations and particularly abundant in species

The History

Mamut Copper Mine in Ranau

Ranau, Sabah has a rich history that dates back many years. The area was originally inhabited by the indigenous Dusun people, who lived in harmony with the surrounding natural environment. The Dusun people had a deep connection with the land and relied on agriculture for their livelihoods.

In the early 20th century, Ranau experienced significant changes with the arrival of British colonialists. The British recognized the strategic importance of the region and established a presence in Ranau. They introduced new systems of governance, infrastructure development, and economic activities.

During World War II, Ranau played a significant role in the infamous Sandakan Death Marches. In 1945, the Japanese Imperial Army forced around 2,400 Allied prisoners of war(POW) to march from Sandakan to Ranau. The march was grueling and brutal, resulting in the death of the majority of prisoners due to exhaustion, malnutrition, and mistreatment. Today, the Kundasang War Memorial stands as a solemn tribute to the fallen soldiers who endured immense suffering during this dark period of history.

Following the war, Ranau began to rebuild and recover. Ranau has also become a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world who come to experience its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. The town's historical significance, combined with its stunning landscapes and friendly community, make it a captivating place to explore and learn about Sabah's past.

As you explore Ranau, you'll discover traces of its history woven into its vibrant present. From the remnants of colonial architecture to the traditions and stories passed down through generations, Ranau's history serves as a reminder of the resilience and spirit of its people

Earthquake

The 2015 Sabah earthquake struck Ranau, Sabah, Malaysia with a moment magnitude of 6.0 on 5 June that year, which lasted for 30 seconds. The earthquake was the strongest to affect Malaysia since the 1976 Sabah earthquake. Mount Kinabalu was closest to the epicentre and was affected by massive landslides causing eighteen people to lost their lives, three of which were from Ranau.
